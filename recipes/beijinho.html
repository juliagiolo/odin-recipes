Beijinho de Coco

Description

Beijinhos are a delicious and easy to make Brazilian dessert. They are Brazilian coconut balls - essentially cocunut fudge rolled into bite-sized balls. The direct translation of the name is coconut kiss. They are great for parties!

Ingredients

  • 1 can of sweetened condensed milk
  • Unsweetened, shredded coconut or cocunut flakes
  • Unsalted butter
  • Heavy cream

Steps

  1. Combine condensed milk, butter, and heavy cream in a medium saucepan over medium heat and heat it up until the butter melts.
  2. After the butter has melted, add half of the shredded coconut to the pan and stir until combined. Keep cooking over medium heat, continuously stirring. Around 10 minutes.
  3. Pour the hot cocunut mixture into a plate, cover with platic wrap to contact, and let it it cool completely. Should take about 2 hours.
  4. Pinch off some of the dough and roll into a small ball; place on a plate. I recommend lightly buttering your hands before this step. Repeat with remaining mixture, then roll each beijinho in shredded coconut.
  5. Store the coconut fudge balls in a cool place or in the refrigerator until serving time.
